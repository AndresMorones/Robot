Library Cut — Visual Comparison

A side-by-side mockup of the Acme Logistics ops console with five candidate libraries (nuqs, @tremor/react, react-day-picker, date-fns, @radix-ui/react-popover) versus a hand-rolled equivalent. Each section shows what you keep, what you lose, and how visible the difference actually is. Total potential savings: ~62 KB gz.

1. KPI Card — Tremor BadgeDelta + SparkAreaChart vs. custom

The delta pill and sparkline render almost identically. Tremor adds ~10 KB gz for two components we can replicate in ~30 lines of JSX + an inline SVG.

What you keep either way: React 19, Next 15, Tailwind 4, shadcn Radix primitives (Dialog / Tooltip / etc.), Recharts. The Recharts library alone (~95 KB) is unaffected by these cuts — it dominates the bundle and is needed for every chart on the dashboard.
Verdict: The biggest single win is react-day-picker at 25 KB — but it's also the cut with the most visible cost. The other four are mostly free wins.

Recommendation

Cut nuqs, Tremor, and date-fns (~25 KB) — visually free. Keep react-day-picker + Radix Popover unless dashboard polish is non-negotiable for the take-home demo.
