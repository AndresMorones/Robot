04 · Step chart · daily cadence

$16,800 · 7d

Step function with subtle area fill. Each day "holds" its value across its slot before stepping. Excellent at making zero-days obvious — they crash visibly to the baseline.

05 · Above / below mean bars

avg $2,400 · 7d

Bars colored against the period mean. Days at-or-above mean are forest; below-mean days are slate; the dashed clay line is the mean reference. At-a-glance "good / weak" pattern.

Forest above mean · Slate below mean · Clay #92400e dashed mean line.

06 · Bars + cumulative overlay

cum $16.8k · 7d

Daily bars (dimmed forest) with a navy running-total line on a secondary right axis. Stakeholder gets both "today" and "to-date" without switching widgets.

Forest-deep bars · Navy-shifted #3b82f6 cumulative · dual Y-axis.

07 · Horizontal bars · ranked rows

$16,800 · 7d

Rotated layout. Day rows on the left, value labels on each bar end. Useful when ranking days matters more than chronology, or for longer (14–30 day) windows that scroll.

Forest bars · inline mono value labels · slate $0 stub bars.

08 · Heatmap strip · density read

$16,800 · 7d

Each day is a tile colored by revenue intensity. Excellent for 30-day windows where bars get cramped — and zero-days are a distinct empty cell rather than missing data. Shows distribution at a glance.

Forest ramp #14532d → #22c55e · empty cells = slate-tone with border · inline $ labels auto-contrast.

$16,800 · 7d

Thin vivid bars sit on top of a translucent 3-day rolling min/max band. The band smooths volatility for the eye while the bars preserve exact daily values. Reads as "context + signal."
