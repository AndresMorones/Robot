ACME Logistics — Wordmark Exploration · Batch A

Pure typography concepts. No icons, no graphic marks. Each wordmark renders inside a simulated dashboard header strip (page title left · wordmark centered · date picker right) on the production near-black background.

Concept A1 — Industrial Black
ACME LOGISTICS

Inter 900, all-caps, +0.32em tracking, single tone (#e6ebf2). Maximum weight, maximum spread — feels like a steel placard bolted to the header. Authoritative and modern; the wide tracking earns it presence without needing color or accent.

Concept A2 — Split Color (DOT Orange)
ACMELOGISTICS

Inter 800, all-caps, +0.22em tracking. ACME in ink, LOGISTICS in DOT-spec orange (#F97316). Two-tone split signals "freight" without an icon — orange is the regulated road-sign hue every dispatcher recognizes. Brand half stays neutral so it sits politely next to data.

Concept A3 — Bebas Stencil Tower
ACME|LOGISTICS

Bebas Neue, tall condensed caps at 32px with +0.24em tracking, separated by a thin orange pipe. Reads like stenciled paint on a trailer door — military / freight-yard vernacular. The pipe is the only allowed accent and acts as a subtle visual break instead of whitespace.

Concept A4 — Couture Tracked Light
ACME—LOGISTICS®

Inter 300, all-caps, extreme +0.42em tracking, em-dash spacer, tiny ® mark. The opposite of A1 — luxury-fashion lightweight with breathing-room kerning. Whispers "established enterprise" rather than shouts. The ® adds quiet legitimacy without any graphic flourish.
