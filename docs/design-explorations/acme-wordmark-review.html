Top-of-dashboard ACME LOGISTICS wordmark

Pure typography only — no icons, no graphic marks. Each wordmark renders inside a simulated dashboard header strip (page title left · wordmark centered · date picker right) on the production near-black background, so you can judge each concept in true context.

Batch A: Typography-forward (4) Batch B: Color & treatment (3) Batch C: Structural / utility (3)
Batch A — Typography-forward
Concept A1 Industrial Black
ACME LOGISTICS

Inter 900, all-caps, +0.32em tracking, single tone. Maximum weight, maximum spread — feels like a steel placard bolted to the header. Authoritative; the wide tracking earns it presence without color or accent.

Concept A2 Split Color (DOT Orange)
ACMELOGISTICS

Inter 800, ACME white + LOGISTICS DOT-orange. Two-tone split signals "freight" without an icon — orange is the regulated road-sign hue every dispatcher recognizes. Brand half stays neutral so it sits politely next to data.

Concept A3 Bebas Stencil Tower
ACME|LOGISTICS

Bebas Neue tall condensed caps, +0.24em tracking, thin orange pipe. Reads like stenciled paint on a trailer door — military / freight-yard vernacular. The pipe is the only allowed accent and acts as a subtle visual break.

Concept A4 Couture Tracked Light
ACME—LOGISTICS®

Inter 300, all-caps, extreme +0.42em tracking, em-dash spacer, tiny ®. The opposite of A1 — luxury-fashion lightweight with breathing-room kerning. Whispers "established enterprise" rather than shouts. The ® adds quiet legitimacy.

Batch B — Color & treatment
Concept B1 Slate → Ember Gradient
ACME LOGISTICS

Bebas Neue with cool-to-warm gradient. Sweeps from slate on the left into DOT-orange on the right edge — reads as neutral information at first glance, with a subtle thermal "active terminal" energy that ramps toward the date picker.

Concept B2 Mid-dot Two-tone
ACME·LOGISTICS

Bold ACME · muted LOGISTICS with a single DOT-orange interpunct as the only color accent. Establishes a visual hierarchy (brand → category) and feels native to ops/CLI tooling without any graphic element.

Concept B3 Outlined in Navy Pill
ACME LOGISTICS

Stroked (transparent-fill) wordmark sitting inside a faint deep-navy pill with a hairline slate border. ACME stroked in white, LOGISTICS stroked in DOT-orange — gives the brand a contained, badge-like presence without an icon.

Batch C — Structural / utility
Concept C1 Terminal Cursor
>ACME LOGISTICS

JetBrains Mono uppercase with an orange `>` shell prompt and a blinking block cursor. Reads like a live ops terminal — instantly signals "internal tool, real-time, engineer-grade".

Concept C2 Stacked Block
ACME.L O G I S T I C S

Big condensed Bebas Neue "ACME." with a tracked-out micro "LOGISTICS" subtitle below. Two-line lockup keeps it inside the height budget while feeling brand-monumental — DOT-orange period anchors it.

Concept C3 Tagged Identifier
ACME_LOGISTICSPROD

Snake_case wordmark with an orange underscore and a small bordered "PROD" environment chip. Devops/internal-tool DNA — reads as a service name in a stack, perfect for an ops console.
