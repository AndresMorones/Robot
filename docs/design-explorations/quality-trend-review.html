Quality Score Trend — 10 concepts

Ten chart-design explorations for the Call Health Score (CHS) trend widget on the Quality tab. Three batches: conventional scatters, multi-dimensional scatters, and alternative chart families.

Batch A

Conventional scatters

Concept A1

Traditional Clean Scatter tier-color · MA-7 trend

< 70 flagged 70 – 85 warn 85 – 100 excellent 7-day moving avg

Standard time-vs-CHS scatter with dashed pass / excellent thresholds and a 7-day centered moving-average ribbon for drift. Tier-colored points read instantly: brick fail, amber warn, forest pass.

Concept A2

Dense Dot-Plot with Jitter swarm · density rail

each call (jittered) calls / day

Smaller, semi-transparent dots with horizontal jitter create cluster intensity — overlapping calls form denser blobs. The bottom rail is a per-day count histogram so a slow day is obvious without a trend line.

Concept A3

Radiant Scatter with Threshold Zones traffic-light · glow rings

excellent zone warn zone fail zone

Faint color-coded gradient bands paint the pass / warn / fail regions across the plot. Larger points wear glow rings matching their tier — at-a-glance telemetry where a single brick dot in the red band reads as urgent.

Batch B

Multi-dimensional scatters

Concept B1

Variable-Size Bubble Scatter size = call duration

good ≥85
warn 70–85
flagged <70
⬤ size = call duration (s)

Bubble radius encodes per-call duration (30–600s); color encodes CHS tier. Multi-dimensional, Bloomberg-terminal style.

Concept B2

Deviation Connectors vs 7-Day Rolling Mean volatility view

above mean
near mean (±3)
below mean
7-day rolling mean

Each point connects to a smooth 7-day rolling-mean curve via a thin vertical line. Forest above / brick below / amber near baseline.

Concept B3

Streamgraph-Overlay Scatter volume × score

good calls/day
warn calls/day
flagged calls/day
dots = per-call CHS

Stacked area beneath shows daily call counts per tier (good/warn/flagged). Dots overlay at low opacity composite — volume + per-call quality in one frame.
